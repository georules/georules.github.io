How it Works

MATERIALS

are provided by:

  • Udacity's Intro to Computer Science course*. The materials are designed for 3 months @ 6 hours/week. With the help of codeslam, you will complete these materials in 2 months, and learn more in the process.

  • codeslam free course materials and tools
  • other free materials on the internet

*This course is not affiliated with Udacity. This course is not selling Udacity course materials. Materials are Creative Commons licensed and free to you.

YOU

already know that computer science is important in today's world and want to learn it.

You will need to watch online videos and complete online quizzes prior to class meetings.

You will need to bring a laptop with a web browser installed to class.

CLASS

meetings occur weekly for 2-4 hours. Class will include:

  • reviewing materials
  • extra lectures
  • group activities
  • completing problem sets
Class size is limited to 10 students committed to the coursework.

GEO

the instructor will:

  • lecture on current and related topics
  • provide a schedule for course checkpoints
  • assist in completing problem sets
  • offer personal mentorship
  • hold office hours by appointment
I will get you through this course!

Geo Miller

is the founder of codeslam - exploring the art of code. He is currently serving as a technical advisor to LobbyQ and an open source contributor to Building Blocks, Tributary, and Inlet.

Formerly, Geo was the Assistant Director of the Program in Interdisciplinary Computing, co-founder of FSU DIGITECH, and programming teaching faculty at Florida State University in Tallahassee, Florida. He completed a Masters' in Scientific Computing and Bachelor of Science in Computer Science both at Florida State University.

For his Master's thesis, he studied uncertainty quantification of reactive transport ground water models using FSU’s High Performance Cluster and Oak Ridge National Laboratory's Blue Gene/P. At the College of Medicine, Geo programmed sensors, machine learning algorithms for classification of audio samples, and data analysis systems for locomotor and song activity of the Zebra Finch.
